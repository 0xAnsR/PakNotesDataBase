Choose the sentence with correct punctuation: Please bring the following items a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator., Please bring the following items: a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator., Please bring the following items; a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator., Please bring the following items- a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator.
  1. Please bring the following items a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator.
  2. Please bring the following items: a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator.
  3. Please bring the following items; a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator.
  4. Please bring the following items- a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator.
(0)
اس سوال کو وضاحت کے ساتھ پڑھیں
Explanation
  • Please bring the following items: a pencil, a notebook, and a calculator.
  • A colon (:) is used to introduce a list after a complete statement.
  • “Items” is followed correctly by a list of examples, so colon fits best.

It was unanimously resolved that the parties _____ launching of popular programmes.
  1. Should be unitedly undertaken
  2. Should be unitedly undertake
  3. Should unitedly undertake
  4. None of these
(0)
اس سوال کو وضاحت کے ساتھ پڑھیں
Explanation
  • After “it was unanimously resolved that…”, we use “should + base form of verb.”
  • So the correct structure is: should undertake.
  • “Unitedly” is an adverb and correctly placed before the verb.

Despite ______ tired, she continued working.
  1. Being
  2. Been
  3. To be
  4. None of these
(0)
اس سوال کو وضاحت کے ساتھ پڑھیں
Explanation
  • “Despite” is followed by a verb + ing form (gerund).
  • So the correct structure is: Despite being tired, she continued working.

Choose the sentence with correct subject-verb agreement: Either the cat or the dog were responsible for the mess, Either the cat or the dog was responsible for the mess, Either the cats or the dog were responsible for the mess
  1. Either the cat or the dog were responsible for the mess
  2. Either the cat or the dog was responsible for the mess
  3. Either the cats or the dog were responsible for the mess
  4. Either the cats or the dog was responsible for the mess
(0)
اس سوال کو وضاحت کے ساتھ پڑھیں
Explanation
  • In “either…or”, the verb agrees with the nearest subject (here: dog = singular).
  • Since “dog” is singular, we use “was.”
  • So, the correct subject-verb agreement is singular verb “was” with singular nearest subject.
